Challenge：Establishing online system to generate sales and data management.

Yiminghui Food Factory is a traditional rice noodle manufacturer which serves the customers in the central part of Wuhan and surrounding cities.

It is using the quite tradition business way, they don’t have any online system to manage their sales and products. More than 400 orders are made by telephone everyday.

I worked with them on this 3-month project to tackle the problem by creating a postive online experience. I did comprehensive user research to collect needs and pains points, and then brought ideas to fruition after rounds of validation and iteration.

User research to find problems and gather insights

I spent one week gathering customers’ stories to understand how the factory works and who are their clients are. Observe and interview method was taken during the research.

Currently the daily working process of Yiming Food

· The customer service department calls all customers (300+) to get the order at 3:00 pm, then put the information in the excel sheet and deliver it to manufacturing department.

· The manufacturing department gets started to manufacture according to the orders at 6:00 pm.

· The transportation department checks the orders based on different regions and distributes the orders to drivers in different regions.

· The business department collects the bill from customers according to the total amount of money in excel sheet every 10 days.

Affinity Diagramming

After gathering the data, I put the notes on individual post-it notes and created data buckets in the form of an affinity diagram. This affinity diagram allowed me to naturally group related ideas. After grouping ideas, I created higher level data buckets that encompassed the overall messages and themes of the various groupings. This completed affinity diagram helped reveal my main findings and insights.

Product proposal

After discussing with the developers, we decided that the backend system is supported both on PC and web, based on responsive web design since the it serves the customer service, sales and finance in the company. Considering the difference from traditional e-commerce, the modules of order management, client management and product management are all optimized.

Taking the consideration of the limitation of clients’ age and their concept, and the coverage of wechat usage in China, we decided to design the frontend based on wechat mini app in order to reduce the learning curve of users at the greatest extent.

Prototyping

After validating various concepts with participants and clients, their feedback led me to create and develop prototypes. For both internal system and online shop. I went through 1 round of paper prototyping, 2 rounds of medium fidelity, and 2 rounds of high-fidelity. The low-fidelity prototypes focused on concept validation and simple usability. The medium-fidelity rounds were more concerned with testing usability and flow between screens. Lastly, the high-fidelity prototypes focused on content and visual design.

Usability Testing

During our medium-fidelity prototyping phase, the accountant、customer service、sales person and two of the clients participated in the usability testing. It consisted of 2 rounds with a total of 6 participants. For both rounds, we used Sketch and mockingbot to create a clickable prototype that participants interacted with inside a web browser.

The Final Prototype

For the backg-stage management system, I divided it into four modules——Clients managemt, Order management, Product management and Data analysis. These four modules supports sales person, customer service, product manager and finance seperately.

All clients’ infomation are collected and then assigned to different sales person by manager. So the clients can still be reached even though sales person left.

Considering some clients may prefer the traditional way to make orders, the order list can be filtered by “Paid orders”, “Unpaid orders” and “No-order cilents”. Customers service only have to call “no order clients” and add the orders manually. All orders list can be export into excel sheet to double check with generation and transport department.

For the last ten years, Yiminhui Food Factory has been focused on these four products and made a big sucess. They are planning to develop some other products. The card style makes it easy to understand their main products.

Daily orders ( both paid online orders and unpaid phone orders ) can be checked. Sales volum can be easily compared between products and sales areas. Sales performance is clear and all data can be export directly to support finance accounting.
